Effects Of Deforestation

Deforestation is the destruction of forest by cutting down trees, it leads to habitat loss of many animals .

It takes 17 full grown trees to make a ton of paper.

Causes of Deforestation
  • Procuring land for cultivation
  • Building houses and factories
  • making furniture or using wood as fuel
  • Natural calamities
  • Forest fire and Drought
    • Effects of Deforestation
    Deforestation causes an increase of atmospheric temperature due to increase in concentration of carbon dioxide leading to global warming .It changes the nature of soil , water holding capacity of soil and leads to soil erosion and desertification. It also causes Droughts and floods.Habitat of many animals will be disturbed by cutting down trees.Their survival become difficult .

    Flood

    Floods are a type of natural disaster that can cause heavy destruction to life and property. It is a condition when rainwater accumulates at a place, flooding populated areas. They can also lead to the loss of numerous lives. At times, it can be highly dangerous and can wipe off an entire village or city.

    Global warming

    global warming refers to the general increase in the earth's average temperature, which causes changes in climate patterns across the globe.

    Droughts

    Drought is a long period without rains leading to a severe shortage of water in the region. The soil continues to lose water by evaporation and transpiration. If, however, it does rain at all or the rainfall is very low for a long period, the loss of water from the soil is not made up and hence the soil becomes dry.

    What will happen if we go on cutting trees ?
  • The temperature will increase to a greater extent
  • The chance of natural disaster to occur will increase
  • The water cycle gets disturbed
